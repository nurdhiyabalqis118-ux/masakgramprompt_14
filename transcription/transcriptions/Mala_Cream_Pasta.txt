One thing as Asians love more than cream in our pasta is spice. 
So I combined the two in this spicy, creamy, numbing mala pasta dish.
To make this, I got some garlic, ginger, chilies, Sichuan peppercorns, five-spice powder, and of course some good old Lao Gan Ma. 
I poured hot oil over it, then blended everything into a potent chili oil. 
I mixed this with an egg, cream, soy sauce, and sesame oil, tossed some spaghetti through the sauce, and topped it with spring onions.
This was salty, spicy, creamy, rich, and tinge with a hint of numbing spiciness.
And even though my spice tolerance isn't very high, each slurp made me go back in for just another bite.
