I'm cooking things from faraway places again and this time it's this famous potato cream curry udon from Japan. 
You can find this in quite a few noodle stores these days but the popular one
seems to be from Yamashita Honki in Tokyo but I haven't been there so I'm making it at home.
To start, I boiled my potatoes, mashed it up and blended it with some whipped cream to get a soft and light consistency. 
This turned out almost like a thick foam that tastes of potato and salt. 
I really like it. 
Next, I did a little prep for my curry base with onions, carrots and chicken. 
I then added some water and a block of my favorite brand of Japanese curry
before tossing in some cooked udon noodles. 
I put the noodles in a bowl, covered it with the potato cream and here's the final result. 
I've never tasted the original but I can totally see why this works. 
Oh damn. The cold potato foam cuts through the hot and heavy curry, adding creaminess and salinity and it also gives it a surprisingly fun temperature contrast.
Nice.
