I did something weird to this apple. No, it's not rotten. It's just a regular
apple that's gone through a process to turn it into the most juicy, drippy,
and moist apple I've ever had. All I did was freeze it for one day until it's rock hard,
then slowly let it defrost in the fridge over two days. That's it!
Freezing it breaks up the cell walls and releases all that juice as it defrosts.
So while this texture is a bit spongy and overly wet, if you give it a squeeze,
you'll extract out this clear golden apple nectar that is super sweet and fragrant.
It almost reminds me of a Snapple or FNN sample drink.
This is one of those things that I think has a lot of culinary potential
with how clean and apple-flavored it is. So, would you try this?
