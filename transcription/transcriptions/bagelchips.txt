I'm replacing poplar snacks at home, and this time it's the Olive Young bagel chips.
If you've been to Olive Young in Korea, you might have come across these bagel chips in their food section.
They come in a bunch of flavors, but this honey butter one is the most popular.
Since I'm craving them and I don't live in Korea, I made them myself.
To start, you'll need bagels of course.
I sliced them into these thin pieces, spread them out on a tray,
and baked them for about 15 minutes until slightly crispy.
Then I made the honey butter mixture by combining melted butter, honey,
salt, some optional MSG, and parsley powder.
Next, I brushed the honey butter on both sides of each chip,
and baked them again for another 30 minutes until they're golden.
They came out super crunchy, buttery, and honey sweet,
and I think I got pretty close to the original.
But yes, you might have noticed that mine looks a lot more caramelized,
which I think actually makes them even more flavorful.
Try it!
