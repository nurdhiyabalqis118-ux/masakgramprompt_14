When I come across a food trend using any sort of Asian ingredient, I have to try it out.
This time it's this rice paper croissant that's been getting a lot of love lately,
and the recipe for it is actually pretty ingenious. I started off by mixing together eggs,
butter, milk, sugar, salt, baking powder, and some vanilla and cinnamon for flavor. Then I dipped
rice paper into the liquid, and layered about five pieces together. I then cut this up, rolled it up,
and I know it doesn't look like a croissant right now, but after a bake, here you get this
pastry that kind of looks like a croissant if you squint hard enough. So how does it taste?
I actually found it really crispy on the outside, but it's definitely a lot more chewy than a traditional
croissant, almost like a tender mochi. I have no complaints about that though. I think it's quite a
fun, easy pastry to make, and it tastes pretty good.
