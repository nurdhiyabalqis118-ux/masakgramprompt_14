People have been obsessed with Korean salt bread for the longest time and I was recently craving
them, so I made some. I started out with a dough made with bread flour, all purpose flour, sugar,
salt, yeast, water and a bit of butter. I shaped it into a big ball, proofed it, punched it down
and divided it up. I then cut up a block of butter into these logs, then rolled the dough
balls into a teardrop shape before nestling the butter in it. Okay yes, instead of the usual
round boys, I somehow ended up giving mine into these long sticks that looked a bit wrong,
but I'm so glad it still tasted as good and buttery with that signature crispy bottom.
So I'm counting that as a win. So you're probably thinking that the ones you had in
Korea were better and yeah I do wish I was having some in Korea right now. Wait what?
My wish came true!
