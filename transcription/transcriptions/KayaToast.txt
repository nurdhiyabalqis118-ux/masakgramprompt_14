This is my favorite classic Malaysian breakfast of Kaya Toast and Half boiled eggs.
But let's imagine we were having this at a fancy, fine dining restaurant.
The base is the same, hinanese of Bengali style white bread,
but cut into bites sized pieces, toasted until its almost burnt,
as an ode to that classic roti bakar flavor.
This is then topped with a frozen sheet of butter cut to the exact same size as the bread,
and sandwich with another piece of bread.
The Kaya then comes in the form of a jammy, piped wave,
and of course, it's gotta be a generous amount.
And finally, to really up the luxury of it,
and to incorporate the egg element,
the whole thing will be covered with a snowfall of cured egg yolk
that will dissolve into creamy, umami as you bite into it.
The ratio of filling to bread on this is pretty insane,
so it did taste extra rich and flavorful,
and I quite like it.
Are we eating it, please? Thank you!
