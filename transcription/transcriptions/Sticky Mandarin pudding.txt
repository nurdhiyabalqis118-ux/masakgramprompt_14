So it's the end of the Lunar New Celebrations, and I've ended up with just a ton of leftover
mandarin oranges.
Instead of eating them and getting the aval, I thought of a way to use them in a dessert
inspired by sticky, toffee pudding.
I peeled the mandarin and removed the seeds, blended them into a smooth sauce, and then
simmered it in a pan for half an hour.
Then I peeped up but then sugar, added in eggs, the mandarin orange paste, flour, baking powder,
baking soda, and salt.
Pour this into buttocks and bake.
I then made a caramel sauce by cooking together some butter, palm sugar, and cream, spread
this out onto the tray, flip out the cakes onto the sauce, and then pour more caramel
on top.
Bake it to more until the sauce bubbles, and you get this super rich, gooey sticky mandarin pudding.
