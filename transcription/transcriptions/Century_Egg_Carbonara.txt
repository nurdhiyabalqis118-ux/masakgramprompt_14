This is a century egg, and don't hate me for this, but I used it to make a century egg carbonara.
My thought process for coming out with this dish was that since century eggs taste funky and pungent and almost blue cheese-like, it should pair pretty well in a cheesy pasta. 
So I mashed up a century egg and combined it with a regular egg, cheese, black pepper, and also some crispy bacon that I rendered down in a pan. 
Then I boiled up some pasta and tossed everything together. 
Oh you have no idea how excited I was to try this, and at this point it was looking and smelling really, really promising. 
I pasted this up with more cheese and some extra century egg slices to really drive home the message, and I ate the whole thing. 
It was really, really good. 
Some might think that century eggs are sulfurous and smelly, but here it's tempered down by the cheese and bacon and turns it into a really lovely, nicely funky
pasta dish that I'm super excited to share.
