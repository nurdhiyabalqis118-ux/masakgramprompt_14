While I was shopping for groceries the other day, I found one of my favorite Japanese ingredients, mentaiko, or spicy pollack roe.
Whenever I see mentaiko in the menu, it's a sure order for me.
But I've never had it outside of Japanese restaurants, so I decided to buy some and
make pasta with it. 
Back in my kitchen, I squeezed out the mentaiko, mixed it with an egg, cream, soy sauce, cheese, and black pepper. 
I cooked up some pasta and tossed everything together.
Then to really boost up the mentaiko flavor, I copied a Japanese restaurant technique
of mixing mentaiko with mayo, spooned it on top of the pasta, and torched it until it's slightly charred. 
The roe popped like little firecrackers and it's a real flavor bomb. 
It's creamy, tangy, slightly spicy, with a strong seafood umami that I just can't stop drooling over.
