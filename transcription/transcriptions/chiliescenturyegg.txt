I love to smash chilies and century egg because it makes one of the most punchy appetizers I've
ever had. I'm of course talking about the classic Hunan dish of Lei La Jiao. To make this,
I got some century eggs and green chilies. You can use any but I got a combination of
spicy green chilies and these bigger sweeter ones. I torched them over an open flame and peeled
them. Then you'll typically need a hardy stone mortar and pestle which I embarrassingly only
realized I don't own as I was making this but I have this cute suribachi which does the job well
so I put some garlic and salt in, smashed it up, added the chilies, soy sauce, vinegar,
sesame and century egg, smashed it some more and finally I served it up with rice and smashed
the whole thing down. It's so good with rice.
