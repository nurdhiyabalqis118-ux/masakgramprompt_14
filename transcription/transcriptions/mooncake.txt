This is a traditional Chinese mooncake, or is it?
Yes, I combined a mooncake and Gordon Ramsay's favorite dish to make a mooncake wellington!
I started with the classic mooncake dough, made with flour, golden syrup,
lye water and oil, mixed together into a smooth dough.
I then cut the beef, gave it a really quick sear,
and I also prepped the mushroom duxelles and cooked it dry.
Then I started assembling.
I flattened out the dough, cut out a piece of parma ham,
layered it with the duxelles, brushed mustard on the beef,
and topped it with more duxelles, ham and pastry.
Wrapping this was kind of messy, but once I pressed it into the mold and gave it a quick bake,
it came out looking absolutely stunning!
And of course, the beef inside is a beautiful, blushy pink.
I know mooncakes can be a polarizing dish,
so I'm not sure if I made it better or worse here.
But tell me, would you eat this mooncake wellington?
