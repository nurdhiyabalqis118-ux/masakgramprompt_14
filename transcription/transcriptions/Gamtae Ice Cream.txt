This is Kante. It's a currency read as very fine and delicate, but it's packed with the surprisingly savory nutty flavor.
I've just tried it in Korea, but I recently had it again at this fine dining meal I was invited to.
I had a lot of amazing Korean ingredients throughout the meal, but the most memorable one was definitely the Kante ice cream.
So I re-created it. Okay, this time I don't have an ice cream machine, so I made a super plebian no-churn ice cream with whipped cream and condensed milk,
and flavored it with some chopped kante as well as kante powder. I did feel a bit bad using this cheap ice cream technique for such quality ingredients,
but it actually turned out to be really yummy. It didn't amount of it too fast though,
but I tried shaping the ice cream into a disc like this. Then I layered some kante sheets,
cut them into circles, and crisp them on a pan. Then I put everything together and topped it with a miso caramel and more kante.
I think I did a pretty good job here, but this was definitely too much work. So the next day when I was craving for more kante ice cream,
I just scooped it into the bowl, drilled some miso caramel and parallel on top,
and added a crispy seaweed snack just for more fun textures. This was so good, so do some of the
favor and go try some kante.
