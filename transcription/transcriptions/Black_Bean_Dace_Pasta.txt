This is one of the nostalgic flavors of my Malaysian childhood.
It's a can of fried dace with salted black beans.
It's a simple, savory hit that goes well with rice or stir-fried with veggies, but I wanted to see if I could work it into other things like pasta. 
I started with the simple beginnings of a classic aglio olio with oil and chopped up garlic. 
Then I sauteed the shredded fish and black beans, and while that's going, I boiled up my pasta until a minute past al dente because that's just how I like it, don't judge me. 
I tossed it into a pan and served it up. 
It tasted super savory and earthy thanks to all that salted dace and black beans,
and it reminds me a bit of an anchovy pasta type situation.
So I think it's a pretty wonderful dish with a bit of an Asian childhood nostalgia.
