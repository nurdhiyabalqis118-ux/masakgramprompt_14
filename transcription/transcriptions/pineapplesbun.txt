This is a classic Hong Kong ball of bao which translates to pineapple bun.
Ball of bao are usually eaten with butter but since they're known as pineapple buns,
I secretly prefer to have them with pineapple jam.
I made the ball of bao by first kneading together all the dough ingredients to get this milky smooth bun dough.
I also then made a separate dough for the buttery crackly top.
And once the buns are proofed, portioned and rolled into balls,
I flatten out the buttery topping into discs to cover the top of each bun.
Then brush them with a shiny egg wash before scoring and baking.
They turned out into these beautiful buns with a pineapple looking top.
But to make it even more pineappley, I carved up and blended a whole pineapple
then cooked it with sugar and spices until thick and jammy.
The pineapple jam then went in between each bun, turning them into truly pineappley pineapple buns.
