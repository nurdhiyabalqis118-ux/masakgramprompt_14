When it comes to snacks, there's nothing quite like super ring.
It's this Malaysian snack that is neon orange and it's supposed to taste like cheese,
but honestly, it's just weirdly sweet, savory, and funky, which maybe is why it speaks to me.
But in my adulthood, there's a tang of guilt that comes with eating a whole bag of super
ring on my own, so I worked it into a very adult dish of chumuk bab, or Korean rice balls.
I crushed up the super ring, then mixed it into cooked rice, seaweed, sesame seeds,
and sesame oil. I then cooked up some ground beef bulgogi, wrapped it in the rice, and rolled them
into balls. It was surprisingly easy to make, and although the super ring lost most of its crunch,
you get a hint of cheesy umami speckled throughout each rice ball that I think is
kinda special. And it definitely tugged at the heartstrings of the little Malaysian kid in me.
