Juk or porridge is the most comforting Chinese dish there is, and I love my juk with bits of crispy,
or chinise cruellers for extra texture, but I didn't have any around, so I made some changes
to the classic with this porridge pie. Okay before you judge me for this, hear me out. This
starts just like a regular porridge, so you know, it'll taste just as comfy and nourishing.
I even made chicken stock from scratch, boiled my rice in it, then added in the classic shredded
chicken, abalone, because I was feeling fancy, and then some ginger and spring onions.
I season it with soy sauce, sesame oil, you know, the regular, then in this move of
ingenuity or insanity, I thought to cover my juk with frozen puff pastry. I egg washed it and
baked it off until the top was brown, and tried my crazy creation. And I burned my tongue off because
it was too hot. Okay, but after it cooled down, I had it again, and it's kind of good. kind of like it.
You definitely get extra texture to the classic juk, but a bigger reason I like this is because of the
straight-up deception in this dish. I'm looking like a pie, but tasting like a juk. Nice.
