I love using Asian flavors in places where people least expect it,
but where it makes total sense after you try it.
In this case, I made a baked potato stuffed with a Japanese salmon filling.
As some of you might say, it's giving onigiri, but with potatoes in place of the rice.
To make this, I took a few potatoes, rubbed them in garlic oil and salt,
and baked them for an hour. I then cooked some salmon,
flaked it up, and mixed it with Japanese mayo and chopped spring onions.
When the potato was done, I cut into it and fluffed it up while it was still steamy,
then I heat down the salmon flakes and topped it with tobiko.
This was actually really easy to make and I know it sounds like high praise,
but I really think it might be the best thing I've made so far this year.
