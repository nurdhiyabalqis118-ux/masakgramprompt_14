I made this jiggly, wobbly, light and airy Japanese, Taiwanese, Portuguese castella cake.
And so can you, because it only uses five ingredients you probably already have at home.
To start, I combined melted butter and milk, warmed it up, then mixed it with cake flour, egg yolks, vanilla, a pinch of salt, and whisked everything until smooth.
I then whipped egg whites and sugar together into a fluffy meringue, then gently combined everything together.
I poured the batter into a pan, set it in a water bath, then baked this for about an hour.
Do all this and you'll get this beautifully soft, jiggly, pillowy castella cake with that satisfyingly sweet melt-in-your-mouth texture.
Mmm, so good!
